HRV2 is an Internet platform for the permanent monitoring of ambulatory cardiovascular patients. The monitoring is carried out from parameters derived from biometric signals, such as heart rate and level of activity, obtained from commercially available wearable devices (e.g. smartwatches). The sensor information is sent to an artificial intelligence server through an Android application for smartphones. The AI server combines advanced techniques for signal processing and artificial intelligence to transform the raw signals into medical parameters that characterize the health state of patients. These parameters are used to monitor, online, the patients’ health state as well as to estimate their acute cardiovascular risk, i.e. the risk to suffer a dangerous cardiovascular event in the next following hour (e.g. dangerous arrhythmias or sudden cardiac arrest). According to this risk estimation, an emergency protocol is triggered that coordinates actions that should be taken by the patient, by a responsible relative of the patient, and by the patient's clinician. The instruction of actions are sent to the smartphones of the patient, relative, and clinician through Android applications and SMS. The clinician has access to an interactive webpage that permits exploring the health history of the patient, the risk estimations, and the triggered emergency protocols. Biometric parameters of the patient are registered using wearable technology.

Action instructions are sent as text messages to the smartphones of an assigned relative and of the patient.

The AI engine permanently monitors the health state of patients and triggers emergency protocols when a high acute risk is detected.

The doctors monitors the patients’ health parameters and the overall performance of the platform through interactive web interfaces.

This panel shows the history of health events of the patients as well as the history of alters sent to the patient, family and doctor.

“Pause” button: pauses the AI engine (and the patient simulation, in case the patient is simulated).


“Delete” button: deletes patient recordings from the central database.


“Edit” button: changes patients basic health parameters (body mass index, age, etc.).


“Create new patient” button: opens a web interface to create a new patient.

This panel shows the instructions generated by the artificial intelligence engine that sends specific alerts to the patient, family, and doctor according to the estimated acute risk.
